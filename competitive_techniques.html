What Are Competitive Techniques?

Competitive Techniques are a series of actions that define the baseline for Competitive Melee. These can include a series of button inputs or just understanding how to move your character around a stage. Below is a small list of the techniques that are used in every/most matches of SSBM.

Top Five Techniques:

  1. Dash Dancing is the most fundamental technique to have your character move fluently on the ground. This technique lets you cancel your dash animation to dash another direction. Example here.
  2. Short Hopping is a mechanic where your character will jump half of the height compared to a full hop. To input a short hop you need to press and let go of the jump button within the frames of a jump squat. This technique is character dependent. Example here.
  3. Wavedashing is what lets SSBM be the most precise 2D platformer fighting game of all time. Many other games have copied what wavedashing does and how it works. To input it you must air dodge into the ground before your character touches the ground to either land faster or slide a distance. Example here.
  4. L-canceling is another technique that creates the competitive standards but is usually learned a little later because first you must know how to move around in the game. L-cancelling lets you cut the endlag of aerials in half. With this technique you are able to perform the combos that professional players are seen doing at tournaments. Example here.
  5. The final technique to learn is a combination of 2 of the other 4 techniques. SHFFL is an acronym for Short Hop Fast Fall L-Cancel. To perform this technique you will short hop then fast fall out of the short hop next perform an aerial finally L-Cancel the aerial. In every modern competitive set you will see this technique being used. It is the core of how to combo your opponent which is the most unique aspect of SSBM. Combining SHFFL and the other 4 techniques above is what is required of every competitive player in order to compete at smaller tournaments and win super majors. Example here.
