Why have rules for Competitive SSBM?

As with any sport like football soccer ect. there are rules to the game. In Super Smash Brothers there is a options menu that lets you change settings to modify the games rules. Examples of those rules are to have items turned on at certain spawn rates (low medium high) turn off/on certain stages add a timer and also change the gamemode. So if you are playing any smash game casually you can play by your own "house" rules but in order to standardize the experience for a competitive scene the community created a ruleset. This ruleset has some interesting history behind it so if you are curious you can look in History for the specifics.


What are the rules for Competitive SSBM?

Like any sport or game there are rules that you need to follow in order to standardize the experience across different tournaments.

Here is a run down of the rules:

Stagelist:

Battlefield
Dream Land N64
Final Destination
Fountain of Dreams
Yoshi's Story
Pokemon Stadium

1-on-1:

  • Sets are played in either best 2 out of 3 in early parts of bracket or best 3 out of 5 in later parts of bracket or top 8.
  • Double Elimination.
  • Each match is played with 4 stocks (lives) and an 8 minute timer.
  • Items are turned off.
  • Pause is turned off.
